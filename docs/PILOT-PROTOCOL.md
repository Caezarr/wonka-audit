# Pilot Protocol

## Cohort

- 10–30 voluntary participants;
- at least two roles and two supported AI tools;
- no team-level reporting below five participants;
- baseline window of 30 days and one comparable checkpoint.

## Acceptance gates

- at least 90% of invited users complete the local run without support;
- zero raw prompt, path, secret or participant identifier leaks in public bundles;
- source status and capability are understandable to at least 80% of participants;
- false-positive review on 100 randomly sampled classifications;
- at least 80% of participants correctly describe the score as directional;
- deletion and share revocation tested by every public-share pilot user.

## Evidence to collect

- source availability and failure reason counts;
- completion time and support requests;
- participant comprehension survey;
- expert review of prompt/category and validation detection;
- score distribution by tool and role, only above privacy thresholds;
- qualitative usefulness of recommended next actions.

## Stop conditions

Pause the pilot for any raw-content leak, reidentification incident, unexplained tool bias above 10 score points, or use of individual scores in a performance process.
